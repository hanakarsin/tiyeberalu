    Baringo: Schools reopening in crisis after several institutions get submerged in lake water

    Learners in Baringo whose schools have been submerged by water from the raging Lake Baringo and Bogoria will have to cope up with a new normal as schools reopen after 10 months of closure.

    With no facilities in place to accommodate them, the pupils and students now face a new unpleasant reality with the government turning a blind eye on their predicament.

    Over 3,000 learners affected

    In this, over 3, 000 learners were affected in the process.

    Twelve primary, five secondary and 22 early childhood institutions were documented to have submerged in the waters. Plans by the government to rehabilitate the schools have since hit a dead end.

    In August 2020, government officials led by Education Principal Secretary Belio Kipsang promised that by the time schools reopen the affected schools would have been relocated.

    TUKO.co.ke however, has established that very little has been achieved forcing learners to share the available space.

    Sharing of classrooms

    At Ngambo primary school for instance, the institution that was fully submerged was relocated to Serian ECDE Centre with a single classroom and learning has turned chaotic.

    Grade Four learners have had to bear the brunt of the scorching sun even as dust swirled around them.

    The pupils cannot concentrate as bleating goats roam freely within and around the ‘classroom’.

    Pastoral instruction (PPI) pupils and Class Eight learners also share a single room, initially an ECDE centre. The struggle was real as each teacher tried to capture the attention of her learners.

    Sharing the same board and the learners sat on different rows, the classroom was all noisy. Each teacher directed her attention to her pupils, unfortunately sounds travels in all direction and the ear could not pick what a learner was interested in.

    It, however, emerged that only 15 pupils out of the total 312 learners of Ngambo primary reported to school. Of the 15, only two were candidates.

    The school headteacher Parkolowa Shariff they were forced to combine the learners for effective teaching.

    In April 2020, floods in the region swept away a police lorry with 12 officers on board.

    The tragic incident happened at Chemoi area in Baringo county on Thursday, April 30, at around 7:30pm.

    A related incident was witnessed in Chesogon area where floods swept through the village killing at least 15 people including two police officers.
