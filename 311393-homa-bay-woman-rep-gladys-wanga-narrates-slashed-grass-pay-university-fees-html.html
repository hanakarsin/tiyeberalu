    Homa Bay Woman Rep Gladys Wanga narrates how she slashed grass to pay university fees

    26 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Homa Bay Woman Representative Gladys Wanga moved audience during Kenyatta University (KU) graduation ceremony with an emotional narration capturing her campus life.

    The former KU students' leader recounted how she joined the school for undergraduate studies and was not sure she would complete her university education.

    As an orphan, Wanga's main challenge would be how to fund her studies and indeed it was a stark reminder that fees would be a challenge considering her late parents were peasants.

    Born and raised in Kano, Kisumu county, a region that largely boasts of small scale rice and maize farming, her academic journey was that which heavily relied on agriculture.

    Her father, John Nyasuna Wanga, was a councillor in the defunct Kisumu city council.

    Even though she did not clarify when her parents passed on, it was evident on her face that their demise served her a heavy blow that nearly crippled her studies.

    As a university student between 2000 and 2004, she would work in cyber cafes and often slashed grass within the campus to augment the little she got from the education loans board.

    "I joined KU in 2000 as an orphan unsure how I would complete education. Thanks to work study programme, I worked in the cyber and even slashed grass under the programme to supplement for my fees some of which I got from HELB (Higher Education Loans Board)," she narrated.

    Despite the financial distress, she graduated with a degree in Appropriate Technology and later enrolled for Masters in Health Management in the same institution.

    "KU prepared me for the leadership position I hold today and rumble and tumble of elective politics. I got the opportunity to sit in the university senates and council," she added.

    The ODM loyalist who was nominated unopposed in 2017 and virtually went on to win the General Election with nearly 90% votes, challenged graduates to be bold in the market.

    The former Parliamentary Service Commission member is currently rumored to be eyeing the Homa Bay county gubernatorial seat in the 2022 poll.
