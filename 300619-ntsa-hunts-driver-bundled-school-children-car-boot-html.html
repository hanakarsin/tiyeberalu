    NTSA hunts for driver who bundled school going children in car boot

    - The car with registration number KBF 383W was seen carrying at least six pupils in its boot - Several others could be seen tightly squeezed at the backseat of the same vehicle - It was not immediately clear where the vehicle was headed to or which school the pupils were from

    05 Jul 2024, 00:00

    - The car with registration number KBF 383W was seen carrying at least six pupils in its boot

    - Several others could be seen tightly squeezed at the backseat of the same vehicle

    - It was not immediately clear where the vehicle was headed to or which school the pupils were from

    - NTSA assured members of the public action would be taken against the driver

    The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) has launched a manhunt for a driver and owner of a vehicle that was spotted carrying six school going children in its boot.

    This is after photos of the said car went viral on Wednesday, March 13, and sparked public outrage with a section of Kenyans on social media demanding for action to be taken against the driver.

    Reacting to the public uproar over the manner in which the youngsters were being transported, the NTSA assured members of the public it had taken note of the disturbing incident and promised to track down the culprits.

    "This case has been brought to our attention, appropriate action will be taken.Keep it here for updates on action taken," the Authority tweeted.
