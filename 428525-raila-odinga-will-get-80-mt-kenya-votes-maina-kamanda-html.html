    Raila Odinga Will Get 80% of Mt Kenya Votes, Maina Kamanda

    Jubilee nominated member of parliament Maina Kamanda projects that in the oncoming presidential vote, the ODM party leader Raila Odinga will secure more than 80% of the total votes from the Mt Kenya region.

    26 Jul 2024, 00:00
    • Maina Kamanda was among leaders who accompanied Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga in his two-day tour to Garissa
    • While speaking to residents, Kamanda claimed Raila has more than 80% of the electorate from the Mt Kenya region supporting his fifth presidential candidature
    • The nominated MP stated that with the support, the road to state house was clear for the former prime minister

    Jubilee nominated member of parliament Maina Kamanda projects that in the oncoming presidential vote, the ODM party leader Raila Odinga will secure more than 80% of the total votes from the Mt Kenya region.

    This, he said, will translate to Raila taking the lead in the contest and ultimately being sworn in as Kenya's fifth president.

    While speaking in Garissa, Kamanda suggested that devoid of the support from the vote-rich Mt Kenya region, the former prime minister had been posing a great force to reckon with in the previous elections.

    "You used to be alone. Now we are together. When it's Kamanda speaking you should know it's central Kenya. I want to assure your that 80% of Mt Kenya is already behind him (Raila). From the onset, I used to say we just needed 10% of the support, now we have 80%," said Kamanda.

    Further, Kamanda said that Raila was the best fit to take over from Uhuru, adding that he would set the foundation for a better leadership post the Kenyattas and Odingas domination of the Kenyan politics.

    "We want a new united Kenya. We want leaders who have fought for our country for a long time. We have the 78-year-old Joe Biden in the US, in South Africa, there was Nelson Mandela, here we have Raila whom we want to take over for only five years before leaving," said Kamanda.

    Raila was in Garissa over the past weekend where he met the locals to popularise his candidature next year.

    In his company was a host of politicians including governors, parliamentarians and other state functionaries among them Treasury CS Ukur Yattani.

    Scramble for Mt Kenya

    The vote-rich region has been drawing the attention of the top contenders in the next year's presidential race.

    The vast Mt Kenya bloc boasts of more than six million voters.

    For a long time, Deputy President William Ruto who also eyes the top seat has been camping in the region to mobilise support for his bid.

    He has been making constant visits to the region creating the assumption that he is already a darling to the electorate.

    Raila has also started his visits to the region where he seeks to avert the notion that he cannot win support from the region's electorate.
