    Githu Muigai avoids resignation calls

    - Following a press conference held by various religious leaders from different faiths, the Attorney General ordered the banning of an atheist society that had been registered earlier this month - AG Githu Muigai gave in to their demands to end calls for his resignation

    27 Jul 2024, 00:00

    - Following a press conference held by various religious leaders from different faiths, the Attorney General ordered the banning of an atheist society that had been registered earlier this month

    - AG Githu Muigai gave in to their demands to end calls for his resignation 

    - Despite a pending Supreme court ruling on its legality, the society had already planned a 'no God party' to celebrate their recognition

    The Atheist Society of Kenya is set to be deregistered following a directive from the Attorney General to the Registrar of Societies to immediately suspend the society.

    The AG gave the reason that the society's case was still pending at the Supreme Court where a ruling was yet to be made on its legality in Kenya.

    However, this could have been directed due to pressure mounted on him yesterday on Friday, April 29 after the the Kenya National Congress of Pentecostal Churches and Ministries and the Muslims’ Shuura Council called on his resignation for registering an unconstitutional society.

    According to the Star, Muslims’ Shuura Council chair Abdullahi Abdi also criticised the registration saying it violated the constitution and mocked Kenya’s cultural and social norms.

    Earlier this month, they were registered and received a certificate which had been signed on February 17.

    According to Nairobi News, the society’s president Harrison Mumia said the government should treat all Kenyans equally, and allow them to exercise their freedom of thought and association, provided they are not a threat to order and peace in the country.

    He hailed the historic move while calling on Kenyans to be tolerant of people’s individual beliefs.

    To celebrate their recognition, the society planned a 'No God party' to be held at a club on Nairobi's Kimathi Street.

    The Atheist society has 5,000 members and launched a countrywide recruitment of members who would assist them in calling for the scrapping of religious studies from the syllabus.
