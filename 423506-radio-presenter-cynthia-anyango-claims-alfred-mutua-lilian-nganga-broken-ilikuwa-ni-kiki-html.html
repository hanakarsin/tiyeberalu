    Machakos governor Alfred Mutua and his wife Lillian Nganga parted ways two months ago. The two had dated for 10 years before deciding to go separate ways.

    On Sunday, August 22, Mutua and singer Rayvanny held a joint birthday party and Lillian Nganga, Raila Odinga, media personalities Trevor Ombija, Salim Swaleh, comedians Churchill and Eric Omondi were among the attendees.

    Many among them self-proclaimed president of single mothers Akothee was wowed by the maturity Alfred Mutua and Lillian Nganga have displayed days after break up. She praised the ex power couple.

    But, KBC radio presenter Cynthia Anyango claims their breakup was just clout chasing, insisting they are still together.

    She said she didn't comment on their breakup because she thinks it was just a publicity stunt.

    "Nilijua tu hakuna kitu kama hiyo. Ilikuwa ni kiki (I knew they hadn't parted way. It was clout chasing). Kenyans including some media personalities were all over writing about Mutua's separation. Jamani kwani till when are we going to be deceived? Sasa muache kufuata maisha ya watu wanajifunika shuka moja (Stop following other people's love lives especially if they ever live together)," she said.

    Mutua unbothered by trolls

    The former government spokesperson told off critics sharing their opinions on his failed relationship with Lillian Nganga.

    Speaking for the first time about their break-up, Mutua said he was unbothered by what people out there were saying. During an interview with Classic 105, Maina Kageni tried to persuade Mutua to talk about it, and he cut him short, saying:
