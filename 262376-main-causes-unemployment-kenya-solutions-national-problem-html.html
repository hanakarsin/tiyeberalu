    31 May 2024, 00:00

    The rate of unemployment in Kenya has fluctuated over the past few years. There are many reasons given as an explanation for this phenomenon. Most people blame unemployment to the challenges facing development in Kenya while others attribute it to the rising youth population in Kenya. These are valid reasons, but there is more to this problem than often meets the eye. The following are possible issues that contribute to the high rates of unemployment in Kenya and what can be done about it.

    Causes of unemployment

    The education system of Kenya has been blamed for a long time as being behind the current crisis of youth unemployment in Kenya. There have been reports that the system as is currently set does not train youths and students in schools to be job creators, but instead pumps them with the knowledge that enables them to become job seekers when they are done with schooling. This has led to a lazy crop of young adults who cannot think outside the box to find means of survival. The emphasis on white collar jobs during formal training in schools and colleges has corroded the mindset of the youth who now believe that without securing an excellent employment opportunity they cannot survive.

    The rate of youth unemployment in Kenya is disturbing not because the youth are incapable of working, but because they expect the government to create jobs for them. This mindset has crippled many who struggle to get to the cities when they can create jobs right where they are in agriculture or even informal sectors such as carpentry and other forms of artisanship.

    The education system has portrayed these jobs to be for those who have not gone to school yet they are the primary drivers of the Kenyan economy. If the Kenya unemployment rate 2015 data is anything to go, the education system needs to be revised to churn out students that are self-reliant and not wholly dependent on getting employed. Students need to leave school with skills that they can turn into business opportunities that will help them lead a good life. Fortunately, the education cabinet secretary, Fred Matiangi, has noticed this loopholes in the education sector and has introduced a new curriculum.

    Rural to urban migration

    Each time census is conducted, there is a revelation that a massive number of youth groups in Kenya migrate to urban areas. This is unfortunate because it only leads to strained living as resources are limited. The scenario has made population growth, and unemployment in urban areas continues to rise due to the seasonal nature of jobs in these areas. The fact that people are competing for the same jobs makes it difficult to survive, thus triggering certain vices. The youth would be better advised to stay in the rural areas and develop these areas as they have a better chance of making money here than they ever will elsewhere.

    Types of unemployment in Kenya

    If a solution has to be reached, then it starts from acknowledging and understanding the problem in the first place. The issue of unemployment in Africa and Kenya specifically is not an overnight occurrence, but a gradual set in that was not controlled from the beginning as it should have. Before starting youth programs in Kenya, understanding the problem first makes it easy to come up with an appropriate response. Here are the types.

  • Involuntary unemployment. This occurs when interested people seek jobs but cannot get due to the existing wage bill
  • Disguised unemployment. Occurs when the employed people are more than those required
  • Frictional unemployment. Occurs when people lose jobs and start searching for new ones
  • Residual unemployment. Unemployment affecting people with mental or physical challenges which hinder their hiring chances
  • Structural unemployment. Occurs when the demand for labor goes down due to changes in production like the introduction of machinery.

    • Regardless of the type of unemployment, the reality remains: There is a considerable number of youth seeking employment and more still to leave colleges. Something has to be done if the society is to be saved.
