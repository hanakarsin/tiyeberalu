    The homicide detectives have inched closer to unravelling the mystery killing of a female university student following the arrest of her suspected killer.

    The late Christine Ambani, who was studying at Kiriri Women’s University of Science and Technology in Mwihoko, was found murdered inside a guest house in Githurai, Kiambu county, on Tuesday, July 12.

    An autopsy conducted on her body established she was stabbed in the chest, leading to excessive bleeding, which later caused her death.

    On Tuesday, July 27, the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) detectives pounced on a man believed to be Ambani's boyfriend at the Chokaa area within Kayole.

    On the day of her death, the DCI team said Ambani had left the university in the morning to meet the boyfriend, identified as Innocent Kalio Makokha, in the Githurai area.

    Makokha currently pursues a Bachelor of Science degree in Specialist Needs Education at Mt. Kenya University.

    Booked a guesthouse

    The two lovers booked themselves into a guest house, hoping to spend few hours so that Ambani could sit continuous assessment tests later that afternoon.

    However, the guest house management confirmed the two students changed their minds so that they could spend the entire night.

    During the night, the detectives suspect Makokho allegedly knifed his lover before he logged into her family WhatsApp group, where he asked for money from relatives.
