    Murkomen on receiving end for suggesting lifting partial lockdown was big mistake

    - Senator Kipchumba Murkomen said infections were bound to rise 10 fold because of the lifting of the partial lockdown in Nairobi and Mombasa - The senator said it was a big mistake for the president to ease the travel restrictions since they would pre-dispose the vulnerable to coronavirus

    06 Jul 2024, 00:00

    - Senator Kipchumba Murkomen said infections were bound to rise 10 fold because of the lifting of the partial lockdown in Nairobi and Mombasa

    - The senator said it was a big mistake for the president to ease the travel restrictions since they would pre-dispose the vulnerable to coronavirus

    - However, Kenyans accused him of hypocrisy since he had flouted the cessation of movement order when it was in place

    Section of netizens has blasted Senator Kipchumba Murkomen for terming the lifting of the partial lockdown in Nairobi and Mombasa as "a big mistake".

    The senator said he was worried since many Kenyans had vowed to travel to upcountry, which was predominantly inhabited by the elderly who were susceptible to the virus.

    "Cessation of movement in and out of Nairobi and Mombasa is a big mistake. I really hope I am wrong but lifting Cessation of Movement in and out of Nairobi and Mombasa will multiply the infections 10 times," the legislator said.
