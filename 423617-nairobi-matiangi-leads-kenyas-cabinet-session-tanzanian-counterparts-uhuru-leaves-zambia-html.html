    Nairobi: Matiang'i Leads Kenya's Cabinet Session with Tanzanian Counterparts after Uhuru Leaves for

    Kenya and Tanzania have agreed to implement three memoranda of understanding on mutual cooperation. This followed a ministerial meeting between Kenyan Cabinet secretaries and colleagues from Tanzania on Tuesday, August 24.

    25 Apr 2024, 00:00
    • Kenya and Tanzania signed an MoU on mutual cooperation in sectors of higher education, technology, politics among others
    • Also discussed was the reaffirmation and demarcation of international boundaries between Kenya and Tanzania
    • President Uhuru Kenyatta gave Matiang'i expanded Cabinet role in 2019

    Kenya and Tanzania have agreed to implement three memoranda of understanding on mutual cooperation.

    This followed a ministerial meeting between Kenyan Cabinet secretaries and colleagues from Tanzania on Tuesday, August 24.

    Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i led his Kenyan colleagues during the meeting which sought to address a wide range of issues.

    Matiang'i disclosed that the three MoUs will largely focus on mutual cooperation in higher education, science and technology, political and diplomatic consultations.

    "My cabinet colleagues and I are delighted to engage with our ministerial partners from the Republic of Tanzania in the fourth Session of the Joint Commission for Cooperation between Kenya and Tanzania," said Matiang'i.

    Also to be addressed will be the reaffirmation and demarcation of international boundaries between Kenya and Tanzania.

    "The new spheres of cooperation are a testament of the priority placed by the heads of state, H.E Uhuru Kenyatta and H.E Suluhu Samia on improving bilateral relations and trade," he added.

    In 2019, President Uhuru Kenyatta appointed Matiang'i chair of the National Development Implementation and Communication Committee to supervise all government projects and spearhead implementation of the Big Four agenda.

    As per the president's executive order, the committee will provide supervisory leadership throughout the delivery cycle of all national government programmes and projects.

    “They will monitor and evaluate the follow-up mechanisms for resources allocated for national government priority programmes and projects, to ensure proper utilisation and realisation of the targeted outcomes,” Uhuru said then.

    Matiang'i would be expected to prepare accurate and timely progress reports on Jubilee Big Four agenda for presentation to the president.

    The committee consists of all cabinet secretaries, Attorney General Paul Kihara and Head of Public Service Joseph Kinyua and Treasury CS Henry Rotich as the deputy chairperson.

    "Methinks Uhuru Kenyatta has appointed Fred Matiangi “Chief Minister” to spearhead his legacy projects because he does not trust Williams Ruto who is obsessed with 2022, to faithfully execute the Big Four Agenda. Period." lawyer Makau Mutua tweeted.
