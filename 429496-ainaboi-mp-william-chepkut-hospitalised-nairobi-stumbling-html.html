    Ainabkoi MP William Chepkut Hospitalised in Nairobi after Falling

    Ainabkoi MP William Chepkut has been hospitalised in a Nairobi hospital after falling and sustaining injuries. In pictures shared by different leaders who visited him in the hospital, the leader is seen with bandages on his legs and hand.

    Ainabkoi MP William Chepkut has been hospitalised in a Nairobi hospital after falling and sustaining injuries.

    In pictures shared by different leaders who visited him in the hospital, the leader is seen with bandages on his legs and hand.

    However, it is still unclear what made the politician fall and when he was admitted to the health facility.

    On the morning of Wednesday, October 13, Kapseret MP Oscar Sudi visited the MP in the facility to check on his progress.

    Kuria burned by electric gadget

    In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria had detailed how he sustained third-degree burns from an electric gadget that he was using to warm his body.

    The MP, who is receiving treatment at the Karen Hospital, narrated that a friend advised him to use the electric gadget to warm his feet due to his legs getting numb.

    Speaking to Citizen TV, the vocal Tanga Tanga lawmaker said on the fateful day, his feet literally exploded.

    Kuria said he was informed that he had sustained third-degree burns and has since been through three surgeries following injuries he sustained from the device that cost him KSh 250,000 to acquire.

    "I was travelling and getting numb and tired and so this guy managed to convince me. I thought it was authorised and it was meant to do some blow burning of my feet. My feet exploded and when I came here the following day, they said I had third-degree burns," he said.
