    While most dog lovers may believe that every dog looks perfect, some breeds are undoubtedly more pleasing to the eyes than others. These cutest dog breeds will make you gasp with admiration when you see them. Attributes such as fur density, skin color, size of the eyes, and the shape of ears all contribute significantly towards a dog's looks.

    Dogs breeds are often classified into groups such as hounds, terriers, sporting, non-sporting, toy, working, and herding, among others. The best-looking dogs in the world do not belong to any particular group, although toy dogs make up a significant portion of the cutest dog breeds. Which are the most adorable dogs in the world?

    Cutest dog breeds

    Do you often find yourself smiling when you see a cute puppy? Here is a look at some of the best cute dog breeds in the world that will melt your heart.

    30. Siberian Husky

    The Siberian Husky is a beautiful dog breed with a thick coat that comes in a multitude of colors and markings. Their blue or multi-colored eyes and striking facial masks only add to the appeal of this breed, which originated in Siberia.

    It is easy to see why many are drawn to the Siberian's wolf-like looks, but be aware that this athletic, intelligent dog can be independent and challenging for first-time dog owners.

    29. Cardigan Welsh Corgi

    The Cardigan Welsh Corgi is undeniably one of the best-looking cute dogs. It has short legs and a deep chest. Cardigans are powerful workers with deceptive speed and grace and can weigh anywhere from 25 to 34 pounds, with females at the lower end of the scale.

    They come in several coat colors, from red to the popular blue-merle pattern. The quickest way to distinguish these breeds from their cousins, Pembroke Welsh Corgis, is to check out the hindquarters: Cardigans have tails while Pembrokes do not.

    28. Australian Shepherd

    Are you looking for cute puppies? Look no further than the Australian Shepherds, a medium-sized dog with a solid build and low center of gravity. They grow to about 18 to 23 inches in height and weigh from about 40 to 60 pounds (18 to 27 kilograms).

    Males generally are taller and heavier than females. Aussies live about 10 to 12 years. These dogs have a lush, medium-length coat that is straight or slightly wavy and a feathering on the back of the legs and a generous mane around the neck.

    Coat colors vary and might be blue or red merle or red or black tricolor, all with white or tan markings. Most Aussies have a naturally short tail, but sometimes tails are docked if longer than four inches.

    27. Poodle

    This breed originated in Germany but is widely associated with France, where it is hugely popular. The poodle was developed as a water retriever, and the distinctive clipping of its heavy coat was initiated to increase the animal's efficiency in the water.

    The breed has been used for such diverse undertakings as performing in circuses and hunting for truffles.

    26. Basset Hound

    Bassets are some of the cutest big dog breeds. They are heavy-boned animals with a large body on fairly short legs. Because they are bulky, bassets are slow maturing dogs, often not reaching full size until they are two years old.

    This breed is immediately recognizable by its short, crooked legs, long hanging ears, and large heads with hanging lips, sad expressive eyes, and wrinkled foreheads.

    25. Beagle

    Beagles are commonly tri-color (black, tan, and white), but their coats also come in combinations of black, tan, red, white, brown, lemon, and red-tick. The Beagle's smooth, dense double coat gets heavier during the winter and sheds in the summer.

    Beagles not only have an adorable face, but they are also generally loving and loveable, happy and companionable, making them great family dogs.

    This breed is also an intelligent pack dog who loves the company of other dogs and people. In fact, it was bred to work in packs and is thus happiest when it has company. A Beagle left alone for too long can easily get restless and destructive.

    24. Golden Retriever

    The Golden Retriever is one of the best dog breeds in terms of its looks, abilities, and temperament. The breed is a sturdy, muscular dog of medium size, famous for the dense, lustrous coat of gold from which its name is derived.

    The broad head, with its friendly and intelligent eyes, short ears, and straight muzzle, is a breed hallmark. In motion, Golden Retrievers move with a smooth, powerful gait, and the feathery tail is carried, as breed fanciers say, with a 'merry action.'

    23. Cavalier King Charles Spaniel

    Although these Spaniels are born to be companions, the dog breed retains the sporty nature of other spaniel breeds. If they are not sitting on a lap or getting a belly rub, nothing makes them happier than to flush a bird and then attempt to retrieve it.

    When young, these dogs are some of the most adorable puppies. When grown, they are as athletic as other true sporting breeds and enjoy hiking, running on the beach, and dog sports such as agility, flyball, and rally.

    22. Bichon Frise

    The Bichon Frise is one of the most adorable dogs in the world, as well as one of the most enthusiastic. Ever the entertainer, these intelligent and perky little pups are a blast to play with and have an ultra-soft, cotton-ball coat.

    21. Border Collie

    Considered the world's best herding dog, the Border Collie is a smart, intense workaholic who lives for order and values employment above all else. His focus is legendary, and so is the havoc he can wreak when bored.

    20. Pembroke Welsh Corgi

    Originally bred to herd cattle, sheep, and horses, the Pembroke Welsh Corgi is an active and intelligent dog breed. Easy to train and eager to learn, Pembrokes are great with children and other pets, and you can find them in four different coat colors and markings.

    19.Dachshund

    The Dachshund is one of the best-looking cute small dogs. The breed is known for its long body and small legs, as well as a lively and courageous personality. Dachshunds are smart, but can be difficult to train if things are too repetitive.

    They can also be stubborn and want to do everything their own way.

    18. Great Dane

    While the Great Dane is not the worlds cutest dog, it irrefutably belongs in the top 20. Great Danes have sweet faces, large ears, and huge paws and come in a variety of colors (black, brindle, fawn, mantle, and harlequin).

    They are also renowned for their gentle temperament.

    17. Boston Terrier

    The Boston Terrier is a compactly built, square-proportioned, short-backed, clean-cut dog. This breed conveys the impression of determination, strength, sturdiness, liveliness, and style, with a graceful carriage.

    The Boston retains many of the attributes of his bulldog ancestors, but in a clean-cut package that makes a handy house companion. The short fine coat, with distinctive markings, adds to this breed's elegant appearance.

    16. Shi Tzu

    While the name 'Shih Tzu' means little lion, there is nothing fierce about this dog breed. Bred solely to be companions, Shih Tzus are affectionate, happy, outgoing house dogs who love nothing more than to follow their people from room to room.

    15. Shiba Inu

    The Shiba Inu is one of the fascinating small dog breeds in the world. It was originally bred to flush birds and small game, as well as the occasional wild boar hunt. The Shiba Inu is one of Japan's six native breeds that include Akita (large), Kishu, Hokkaido, Kai, Shikoku (medium), and Shiba (small).

    The Shiba Inu is known for its spirited personality, small upright ears, and cat-like agility.

    14. Dalmatian

    Dalmatians stand out for their unique spotted coats, which are short, sleek, and glossy. The spots can be black or liver on a white background. The Dalmatian's outline is somewhat squarish, making them well-balanced, strong, muscular dogs.

    13. Goldendoodle

    An adorable mix between the Golden Retriever and Poodle, Goldendoodles are the ultimate combination of good looks, smart wits, and playfulness. The hybrids are known as a 'designer breed' because they were bred to have the lovable qualities of both of their parents.

    12. Chow Chow

    Chow Chows look more like stuffed animals than actual pets, and they don't get any less cute as they age. Their ears are particularly fascinating. You could totally miss them if you do not look hard enough.

    This breed has erect ears, a broad skull, and a tail curled up over the back. The trademark characteristic is, of course, the blue/black tongue. The facial wrinkles, often obscured by hair, give this dog a scowling expression.

    11. Cockapoo

    What is the cutest puppy in the world? While the answer may vary from one person to the other, the Cockapoo is undoubtedly one of the contenders.

    An adorable, small dog with the personality of a big clown, the Cockapoo was one of the first 'designer dogs.' The breed dates back to the 1960s.

    Breeders combined the Cocker Spaniel and Poodle breeds to make a completely people-oriented hybrid who is intelligent enough to train easily, hardly sheds, forgives quickly.

    10. Havanese

    The Havanese is a small dog that usually weighs between 7 and 13 pounds. Its height ranges from 8.5 to 11.5 inches. Havanese have drop ears and a tail that curls over the back and generally mature at one year of age, although they can easily reach their full size from six to eight months.

    This dog is often called the 'Havana silk dog' because its coat, while double-coated, feels like fine silk.

    9. Yorkshire Terrier

    Yorkshire Terriers have the trifecta of cuteness, tiny size, big eyes, and pointy ears. The breed is a compact, toy-size terrier weighing no more than seven pounds whose crowning glory is a floor-length, silky coat of fur.

    8. Cairn Terrier

    The Cairn Terrier is a short-legged dog with a short, broad face fixed in a somewhat 'keen' expression that is typical of the breed. Its harsh coat is usually bluish-gray, tan, or pale yellowish-brown.

    Generally active, hardy, alert, and spirited, this breed is valued as a pet and watchdog.

    7. Pomeranian

    The Pomeranian is a small, square-proportioned, miniature Spitz breed with a relatively small, rounded body. This breed shares the Spitz's characteristics of small ears, double coat, and curled tail.

    6. French Bulldog

    French Bulldogs have unmistakable bat-shaped ears and a distinctive bow-legged gait. The breed has gained so much popularity that it is fast becoming the city-dwellers' dog of choice.

    French Bulldogs are often small, weighing just under 28 pounds, and have a short coat that comes in a variety of colors.

    5. Old English Sheepdog

    The Old English Sheepdog is a large, athletic dog breed with an unmistakable shaggy coat. The dog was historically bred as a herding dog to help farmers drive cattle and sheep to the market.

    4. Bearded Collie

    Bearded Collies are medium-sized dogs, ranging in height from 20 to 22 inches and in weight from 50 to 60 pounds (20 to 25 kilograms).

    They are often longer than they are tall, and underneath the coat are very nimble and agile dogs.

    3. Pug

    Pugs are arguably some of the best-known dog breeds in the world. They are easily recognizable by their round heads, square bodies, and curly tails. Wherever pugs are, their wrinkly faces and fun-loving personalities easily charm people.

    They are also good with other dogs, cats, and children, and nothing makes them happier than being part of a family. While pugs are no jogging buddies, they enjoy the occasional an outing to the office or park.

    2. Papillon

    The Papillon dog breed descends from the toy spaniels that are frequently portrayed in paintings from as early as the 16th century. Papillons are highly active and portray amazing agility and obedience.

    1. Shetland Sheepdog

    What is the cutest dog in the world 2020? The top position goes to the Shetland Sheepdog. Also known as the Sheltie, this is a small working dog developed as a herding dog for the small sheep of the Shetland Islands, Scotland.

    The dog resembles a miniature rough-coated collie. Like the collie, the Sheltie is descended from an old breed of Scottish working dogs.
