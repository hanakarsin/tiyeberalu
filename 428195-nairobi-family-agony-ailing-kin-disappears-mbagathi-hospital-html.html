    Nairobi Family in Agony after Ailing Kin Disappears from Mbagathi Hospital

    A Nairobi family is living in agony after its kin went missing two weeks ago and they have not been able to trace her. Saumu Amina Wanjiku is said to have accompanied her husband Salim Ramadhan to Mbagathi Hospital for medical attention before later disappearing.

    • They had left for Nairobi's Mbagathi Hospital where Saumu had gone to seek medical attention
    • She never returned, and her family is still searching for her two weeks later

    A Nairobi family is living in agony after its kin went missing two weeks ago and they have not been able to trace her.

    Saumu Amina Wanjiku is said to have accompanied her husband Salim Ramadhan to Mbagathi Hospital for medical attention before later disappearing.

    Saumu disappears from hospital vicinity

    Ramadhan said he went to the facility's pharmacy to pick medicine on her behalf but upon his return, he could not find her within the precincts of the hospital.

    The husband presumed Amina had left for home and thus left the facility, only to be shocked after he could not find her at home.

    "It was 3pm when we parted ways at the facility. I had gone to the pharmacy but when I got back, I could not spot her in the hospital's vicinity. I stayed around until 6:30 when I assumed she had gone back home. So I also left home but did not find her home," said Ramadhan.

    The family launched a frantic search for her in the city including visiting known mortuaries and hospitals but all was in vain.

    Family conned KSh 12,000

    A week into the search, two strangers from Migori contacted the family, one purporting to be a police officer and the other a medic.

    The strangers both claimed to know the whereabouts of Saumu.

    They convinced the family they had encountered Saumu whose health was said to have deteriorated and something had to be done to salvage her.

    The "officer" who had since then kept in touch with the family asked them to send cash to cater for their kin's needs.

    Out of desperation, the family cooperated, sending the stranger the cash he claimed to channel to Saumu's upkeep.

    "I got four missed calls on the Tuesday of September 21 and when I called back, the person on the other end claimed he was a police officer identified as David and was stationed in Migori. He told me he had encountered a sickly woman who gave him my contact. I then knew she was my mother" said Khadija Njoki, Saumu's daughter."The officer said my "mother" was in critical condition and was on verge of dying if caution was not taken. He would then ask for money which he said was for my "mother's" upkeep which we sent. We were later told the person was a conman," added Khadija.

    Saumu's family had already sent David cash totalling KSh 12,000 when it dawned on them they had been engaging a con.

    The family lodged a complaint with the officers based at the Nairobi's Kilimani Police Station who have launched investigations as they pursue the strangers from Migori.

    It is their hope that they will reunite with their kin whom they trust will be in one piece.
