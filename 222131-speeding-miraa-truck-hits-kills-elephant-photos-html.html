    An elephant was killed by a miraa truck today, Friday, November 18, along the busy Nairobi- Mombasa highway.

    The accident happen in the morning in the busy highway giving the driver of the pick-up injuries but no death was reported.

    Miraa vehicles are known to speed on the highways as they deliver Khat to different parts of Kenya in the shortest time possible to preserve its freshness and value.

    “Miraa cars should not stop anywhere not even for five minutes, miraa is perishable. The way it is packaged, at the bottom it can go bad because of the heat. That’s why we drive fast so that it doesn’t also dry,” a miraa driver (Naftali) explained to Capital FM News in an interview.

    The drivers flaunt traffic rules in the process but rarely get pulled down by police.
