    US election presidential results: Biden takes early lead in tight race as Trump claims election frau

    The polls are closing fast in the US after the Tuesday, November 3, General Election. Election results are trickling-in in quick succession, and at the moment, Democrat Joe Biden appears to be leading.

    08 Feb 2024, 00:00

    The polls are closing fast in the US after the Tuesday, November 3, General Election. Election results are trickling-in in quick succession, and at the moment, Democrat Joe Biden appears to be leading.

    Section of experts had predicted that Biden will start leading based on the assumption that most votes that will be counted first in most states are absentee ballots.

    Most people who participated in early voting were leaning towards Biden's Democratic Party.

    Most of President Donald Trump's supporters chose to vote Tuesday and this is expected to interestingly change the way the electoral map will be shaping itself during the day.

    Among places Biden appears to have more votes than Trump include North Carolina, Ohio, Pennsylvania as Trump leads in Virginia, Georgia, Wisconsin and Florida.

    This is, however, expected to change as more polls close.

    TUKO.co.ke will provide you with insightful and credible live updates of the election as Americans seek to hire their new leader.

    Nov 04 10:37 PM

    Projected results: Biden wins battleground Wisconsin

    Joe Biden has improved his chances after winning Wisconsin, which was considered one of the key battlegrounds that will determine who wins the race to White House.

    Trump's campaign team had, however, indicated they would request a recount in Wisconsin, which is possible under the state's law.

    A candidate needs at least 270 electoral votes to win the race. At the moment, Biden's total number of electoral votes stands at 248 against Trump's 214 going by The Associated Press figures.

    Nov 04 8:47 PM

    Biden breaks Obama's popular vote record as Trump cries foul over vote counting

    US media reports indicate Democratic Party's presidential candidate Joe Biden has received the highest number of votes ever in the country's history. He has over 69.7 million votes so far and counting is ongoing in several states.

    The record was previously held by ex-president Barack Obama who received 69.4 million votes in the 2008 presidential election.

    Meanwhile, President Donald Trump (Republican) continues to cast aspersions on the vote counting process, baselessly suggesting the election is being rigged in Biden's favour.

    "They are finding Biden votes all over the place — in Pennsylvania, Wisconsin, and Michigan. So bad for our Country!" one of his latest tweets reads.

    Nov 04 7:09 PM

    Protests erupt near The White House

    US media reported that protests were witnessed near The White House in Washington DC among other places in the country. The demonstrations were led by "black lives matter" activists.

    At least 40 people were arrested by police in Los Angeles in connection with protests.

    Nov 04 7:01 PM

    Donald Trump insists poll has been rigged

    As the world awaits to see if Republican Donald Trump will move to the Supreme Court to challenge the Tuesday election, the president has maintained that the entire balloting process has been flawed.

    According to trump, he was leading in many states on the night after elections but "illegitimate" ballots "dumped" in polling stations started being counted and this changed the trajectory of the vote in Joe Biden's favour

    "Last night I was leading, often solidly, in many key states, in almost all instances Democrat-run and controlled. Then, one by one, they started to magically disappear as surprise ballot dumps were counted. Very strange, and the “pollsters” got it completely and historically wrong!" trump said in a statement.

    Nov 04 6:57 PM

    Poll officials say result will come today

    As the race to The White House continued to tighten on evening of Wednesday, election officials in the US said they were optimistic the winner of the Tuesday poll will be unveiled today.

    This came as Joe Biden appeared to take an early lead ahead of Donald Trump who has threatened to go to court protesting credibility of the vote.

    Nov 04 12:46 PM

    Electoral College: Biden-224, Trump-213

    Within the past two hours, Joe Biden has moved up four places to 224. He is 11 places ahead of Donald Trump on the electoral college map.

    A candidate that will accumulate 270 electoral votes or more will be the ultimate winner.

    These figures are expected to change as more results continue to trickle in.

    Nov 04 10:21 AM

    Donald Trumps speaks: Say he's headed to Supreme Court to halt vote counting

    Donald Trump has accused the Democratic Party of "disenfranchising voters" and claimed victory of the Tuesday, November 3, poll.

    He said that his party was planning for a big celebration but the mood has been suppressed by news of "deceitful" results.

    "This is a fraud and it is an embarrassment to the American country. We were planning to win this election and frankly, we have won this election...to me, this is a very sad moment," said Trump who took issue with vote-counting in Michigan, Wisconsin and Pennsylvania.

    Nov 04 9:39 AM

    Electoral College: Biden-220, Trump-213

    Donald Trump (now at 213) is quickly moving up. The difference in votes between him and Joe Biden (220) is only seven.

    Nov 04 9:29 AM

    Electoral College: Biden-220, Trump-210

    In just 10 minutes, both candidates have moved up by one place to 220 for Joe Biden and 210 for Donald Trump.

    Nov 04 9:19 AM

    Electoral College: Biden-219, Trump-209

    Donald Trump appears to be catching up with Joe Biden. The gap is closing.

    The Democrat is at 219 while the Republican is tailing him closely at 209.

    Nov 04 9:00 AM

    Electoral College: Biden-215, Trump-171

    As both candidates race to hit the 270 mark, Joe Biden and Donald Trump are at 215 and 171 respectively.

    Biden has said, "it is not in his place or Trump's to declare the winner".

    On the other hand, Trump who is expected to make an address soon has claimed the polls are being rigged.

    Nov 04 8:53 AM

    Donald Trump claims election is being rigged

    As Joe Biden asked his supporters to remain calm and with for tallying to be completed, Trump took to Twitter and claimed: "They are trying to steal the election".

    Trump has claimed that voting continued in some places even after polling closed.

    Twitter has, however, flagged the tweet.

    "We are up big, but they are trying to steal the election. We will never let them do it. Votes cannot be cast after the polls are closed!" Said Trump.

    Nov 04 8:51 AM

    Donald Trump to address US citizens: "A big win"

    Nov 04 8:42 AM

    Joe Biden speaks: "We feel good about where we are. We are on track to win"

    Joe Biden is addressing citizens in Delaware.

    He told his supporters that his party was feeling good about the elections and was optimistic about storming into victory.

    "We feel good about where we are. We really do we believe we are on track to win the election. We believe we are on track to win the election. We, however, need to be patient until the hard work of tallying votes is finished," said Biden.

    He was accompanied by his wife Jill.
