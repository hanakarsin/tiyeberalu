    Celebrated police officer Sammy Ondimu Ngare is very romantic. He always treats his wife, Nellie Ondimu, like a queen. They are blessed with three beautiful daughters.

    Last weekend while many chose to flock to Naivasha for the Word Rally Championships Safari Rally event, the humble cop flew his wife to a special getaway in Malindi during her birthday and 17th marriage anniversary celebration.

    Ondimu, the 'Polisi ni Binadamu' advocate, pampered his better half with love on her birthday, and he said she was a supportive, prayerful and Godfearing woman.

    "Whenever I leave home, your prayer and hope is to always see me home alive again. Marrying a cop needs a strong person. Always away from home and always absent on very many urgent occasions. You are the best my darling. I always wanted a woman who knows and loves God and that's exactly what God gave me," he said.

    The loveable cop also noted that their marriage hasn't been a smooth journey and he thanked her for being understanding.

    "Your tender, love and care is immense. Your support and understanding immeasurable. As they say, the best thing to hold onto is each other. Happy Nellie Ondimu aka mama 3Gs aka Dodoise and Happy 17th Anniversary to us. We love you," he added.

    Profession just like any other

    As earlier reported, Ondimu recently clapped back at a fan who claimed law enforcers were recruited into the service after failing national examinations.

    Ngare had taken to his Facebook to interact with his followers on how he will enforce the new curfew orders made by President Uhuru Kenyatta on Friday, March 26.

    However, a fan identified as Cynthia Chekatah who took issue with his post claimed police officers were full of themselves not knowing that they were recruited after failing in their exams.

    The revered officer who doubles up as a singer, in a quick rejoinder, savagely replied to Chekatah's comment using poor English to resonate with her perceived notion that officers were not learned.
