    Likoni Ferry: Victims family begin to hold vigil as recovery operations continue

    - John Wambua, the husband of Kighenda, said he has been asked to perform rituals for the bodies to be found - Family and friends have been convening for vigils at his home for prayers - Search operation of the bodies of Wambua's wife and daughter have been going on for the past week

    10 Apr 2024, 00:00

    - John Wambua, the husband of Kighenda, said he has been asked to perform rituals for the bodies to be found

    - Family and friends have been convening for vigils at his home for prayers

    - Search operation of the bodies of Wambua's wife and daughter have been going on for the past week

    Eight days after Mariam Kighenda and her four-year-old daughter Amanda Mutheu drowned at the Likoni Channel, family and friends have began meeting for vigils at the victims' home.

    The vigils were earlier being held at the beach, but as the recovery operations proved to take longer, the family shifted them to their home on Saturday, October 6.

    READ ALSO: 11 inspiring photos of siblings Ezekiel and Esther who won East Africa Got Talent

    According to the Standard, Kighenda's sister-in-law, Roselyn Mutavi, said they meet to pray and encourage each other.

    " During our prayer meetings, we sing, pray and cry every day until morning. Crying is an outlet for the pain and it therefore brings healing", said Mutavi.

    The vigils are being led by Pastor Samael Mwangemi and congregants from Winners Chapel Church who are constantly praying for the family.

    John Wambua, the husband and father of the deceased, regrets not being there on the fateful day to save his family when they needed him the most.

    READ ALSO: Nominated Senator Millicent Omanga shakes what her mama gave her during rally

    Speaking in an interview, Wambua said he wouldn't wish the pain he is going through on even his worst enemy.

    He also opened up on the pressure he has been getting from people who have been urging him to perform some rituals so that the bodies of his loved ones may be recovered.

    "Within the eight last days, some people have approached me and asked me to slaughter a cow so the bodies may be retrieved. But I have stood firm on my faith because it is only the blood of Jesus that can save life," said Wambua.

    READ ALSO: David Rudisha narrates struggle after losing father, divorce and surviving car crash

    Kighenda and Amanda drowned on Sunday, September 29 after their car slipped off a moving ferry and plunged into the ocean.

    Recovery operations have been going on for the last one week with no success.

    The government also hired a South African firm to help in the operations.

    A Swedish diver who had claimed to be in the capacity to retrieve the bodies in less than two failed to retrieved the bodies and termed the operation as complex.
