    Embakasi East MP Babu Owino has ridiculed the parties celebrating the decision by the High Court to annul the BBI process.

    A five-judge bench on the evening of Thursday, May 13, declared illegitimate the process through which the bill went through, terming the whole process as unconstitutional. The ruling parked wild celebrations in the Tanga Tanga camp.

    Reacting the celebrations, Babu said it was too early for the anti-BBI team to celebrate the ruling, stating there was still much to be worked.

    "Tanga Tanga celebration is as brief as an ejac*lation. This is politics, and the end will justify the means," he said.

    Soon after the ruling, members of the Jubilee's Tanga Tanga faction erupted into celebrations, praising the judges for "salvaging" Kenyans from what they said was a big-time fraud.

    The judges in their ruling stated that the process to amend the Constitution through BBI had contravened the law.

    "The BBI was the president's initiative which is contrary to Article 257 of the constitution. The task force that morphed to a steering committee was an unlawful and unconstitutional outfit. It was invalid from the beginning," they said.

    The bench was comprised of judges Joel Ngugi, George Odunga, Jairus Ngaah, Chacha Mwita and Teresia Matheka.

    The BBI was a brainchild of President Uhuru Kenyatta and former premier Raila Odinga after reaching a ceasefire in 2018 following the disputes that marred the 2017 presidential vote.

    While seemingly belittling the ceasefire between the two leaders, the court said the president had overstretched his mandate by initiating a constitution change.

    "The president, government or any state organ cannot be an initiator of and an umpire in amendment of the constitution," the court said.

    The court further challenged the assertion that BBI was aimed at unifying the country.

    The judges said there was no transparency in the bill as it was being fronted by an individual, Uhuru, to whom the steering committee was accountable.
