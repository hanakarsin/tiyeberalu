    Kenyan Man Who Faked Death to Defraud Insurance 500k Sentenced to 5 Years in Jail

    A man has been sentenced to five years imprisonment after faking the death of a relative to defraud Britam Insurance over KSh 500,000. The culprit on Wednesday, October 13, appeared before Milimani senior principal magistrate Benard Ochoi, who said the accused had committed a serious crime by seeking to enrich himself through deceit.

    A man has been sentenced to five years imprisonment after faking the death of a relative to defraud Britam Insurance over KSh 500,000.

    The culprit on Wednesday, October 13, appeared before Milimani senior principal magistrate Benard Ochoi, who said the accused had committed a serious crime by seeking to enrich himself through deceit.

    The magistrate said were it not for the keenness of the officers at Britam, the accused would have siphoned the money by faking the death of the policyholder.

    He pleaded guilty to the eight counts filed against him by the prosecution led by Anderson Gikunda.

    In the first count, Chalo was charged with forging a burial permit for one Mutisya Musyoka on March 17, 2021, purporting it to be a valid document issued by Rehema Medical Health Centre.

    The prosecution said on March 19, at Britam Life Assurance Limited, Phoenix Branch, Kenyatta Avenue in Nairobi, Chalo knowingly and fraudulently presented the fake burial permit in a bid to claim compensation.

    Chalo pleaded guilty

    In a report by the Daily Nation, he was also charged with raising a Britam death claim notification for Musyoka.

    Chalo pleaded for leniency, saying he regrets the offence.

    The magistrate slapped the accused with a custodial sentence without the option of a fine.

    “It is very immoral to fake the death of a living person,” the magistrate said.
