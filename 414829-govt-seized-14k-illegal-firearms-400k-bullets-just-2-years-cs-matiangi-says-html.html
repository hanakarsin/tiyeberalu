    Gov't Seized 14k Illegal Firearms, 400k Bullets in Just 2 Years, CS Matiang'i Says

    The Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i has called for collaborative efforts between state agencies and public members to contain rising insecurity and crimes.

    Matiang'i disclosed that the government has managed to collect close to 14,000 guns and 400,000 bullets in two years, thanks to the crackdown on illegal firearms launched in 2018.

    "It helpful for the public to be conscious of rising crime. In two years alone, we have collected about 14,000 guns, over 400,000 bullets from the wrong hands. That says something about where we find ourselves. I'm happy to sit down with everyone. I will not blame anyone, let's work together," he stated.

    "We have invested heavily in tracking down crimes, some of which are very sophisticated. We want to ask the public to collaborate wi us, all these people who are running "wash wash" and money laundering, all these characters who are trying to abduct kids," he added.

    He was speaking at the launch of the Office of the Director of Public Prosecution's Excellence Charter and Standard Operating Procedures on Investigation at Prosecution Training Institute on Tuesday, June 8.

    Disarmament

    In 2018, Matiang'i ordered all licensed gun holders to surrender their firearms for verification to curb increased cases of gun misuse in the country.

    He disbanded the Firearms Licensing Board and appointed new members; he mandated to vet all gun certificates and monitor a centralised electronic register of armed private citizens.

    While noting a lapse in the issuance of guns, Matiang'i directed all civilian firearm holders to appear in person before the board to certify whether or not they were fit to own any gun.

    In disbanding the board, he defended his action saying the move was to pave the way for forming a new and effective agency to monitor the issuance of guns.

    At the time, findings by the Geneva-based Small Arms Survey in July indicated Kenyan civilians owned at least 750,000 firearms in 2018; the number rose from 680,000 in 2016.

    Surprisingly, out of the hundreds of thousands of guns reported being in the hands of civilians, only 8,136, which was equivalent to 1%, are in the government register.
