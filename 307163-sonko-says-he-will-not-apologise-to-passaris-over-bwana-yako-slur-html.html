    Sonko says he will not apologise to Passaris over 'bwana yako' slur

    - Sonko held he will not apologise because he did nothing wrong - The governor said Passaris should be the one to apologise first - Sonko's remarks came just days after the two clashed in public during the Madaraka Day celebration held at Pumwani Police grounds

    09 May 2024, 00:00

    - Sonko held he will not apologise because he did nothing wrong

    - The governor said Passaris should be the one to apologise first

    - Sonko's remarks came just days after the two clashed in public during the Madaraka Day celebration held at Pumwani Police grounds

    - Passaris had taken shots at Sonko who in return demolished her during his speech

    - The woman had claimed the governor does not take her phone calls to which the governor said he was not her husband

    Nairobi governor Mike Sonko has declared he will not apologise for insulting Nairobi Woman Representative Esther Passaris during the Madaraka Day celebrations held at Pumwani Police grounds.

    Sonko, on Monday, June 3, reiterated he will ask for forgiveness from her counterpart because he did not do anything wrong.

    Speaking at City Hall, the flamboyant politician claimed Passaris's aim was to create a rift between him and Kenyan women.

    ''She just wants to create a rift between me and women and I love women. One woman should not play diversionary tactic to make women hate me. I have a wife and children who will be women tomorrow',' Sonko said.

    The governor said he respects and values Passaris because she has a good development plan for Nairobians but will not apologise to her, but instead she should be the one to do it.

    "I didnâ€™t abuse her. I told her I am not her husband, which is true. Why I should apologise for speaking the truth? If she apologises first, then I will apologise," he added.

    Sonko's remarks came just days after the two clashed in public during the Madaraka Day celebration held at Pumwani Police grounds on Saturday, June 1.

    Trouble started during speeches when Passaris took to the podium to address the gathering but somewhere in the end, accused Sonko of sidelining other leaders in running of the county affairs.

    The vocal woman rep claimed the governor often snubbed her phone calls regardless of urgency of the matter at hand.

    She also urged him to mend ties with Starehe MP Charles Njagua alias Jaguar, saying their frosty relationship was hurting Nairobi residents.

    Passaris further criticised the county chief for taking relief food to hunger-stricken Turkana, arguing the residents of his own county hardly put food on the table.

    Visibly Irked by the accusations, Sonko, who is known for his carefree attitude, responded to every allegation and he had no kind words for the county MP, including that he was not the woman rep's husband.
