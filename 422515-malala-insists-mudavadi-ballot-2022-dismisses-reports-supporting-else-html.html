    Kakamega Senator Cleophas Malala has dismissed reports indicating that ANC party leader Musalia Mudavadi will pull out of his presidential quest and support another candidate in the 2022 General Elections.

    • He dismissed reports claiming that Mudavadi will pull out of the race and support someone else
    • Malala said the ANC party leader is not going for something less than the presidency in the forthcoming elections

    Kakamega Senator Cleophas Malala has dismissed reports indicating that ANC party leader Musalia Mudavadi will pull out of his presidential quest and support another candidate in the 2022 General Elections.

    Mudavadi to pull out of the race

    According to Malala, Musalia has been endorsed by the ANC party for the top seat, and that has not changed.

    The Senator told off those expecting Mudavadi’s endorsement, adding that they will ensure he gets into the ballot as a presidential candidate.

    “There has been widespread speculation that our party leader will be supporting somebody else in 2022 General Election. We want to affirm here that as Amani National Congress, we gave him the mandate to go and represent us as a presidential candidate; not as a campaign manager, not as a deputy president, not as a prime minister but as a presidential candidate. That is our position,” Malala said.

    Mudavadi is the best presidential candidate.

    The Kakamega senator exuded confidence that Mudavadi is the right candidate for the seat, adding that he is hopeful he will emerge victoriously.

    Mudavadi, a member of the One Kenya Alliance, is hoping to get the endorsement of his co-principals, including Wiper’s Kalonzo Musyoka, Ford Kenya’s Moses Wetangula and KANU’s Gideon Moi.

    Kalonzo is equally upbeat that he will be a presidential candidate in the 2022 General Election

    This comes even as the ODM leader Raila Odinga is reportedly in discussion with OKA leaders for an alliance about the 2022 polls.

    The reports came against the backdrop of calls to have the position party unite to have solid ground to face Deputy President William Ruto, who is believed to be a frontrunner.
